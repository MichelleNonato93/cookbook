Folks, this is killer. For what this cobbler lacks in beauty it makes up ten-fold in taste, especially after a full day on the move. Not much mise en place when it comes to camp cooking, and I wouldnâ€™t want it any other way. Any summer fruit combination will do. I had strawberries and rhubarb on hand for this trip. I imagine peaches, plums, cherries, or blackberries will be fantastic as they come into season this summer.

At home: Mix together the dry ingredients, except sugar, and place in a bag or tupperware. Pack sugar separately. Measure out the buttermilk and transfer to a small container.

  • 8 tablespoons (1 stick) unsalted
    butter, cut into small pieces
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 lbs strawberries, hulled and halved
  • 2 stalks rhubarb, sliced
  • 3 cups flour (I used pastry)
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups buttermilk
